Development & Inspiration

Project Development

Our project started with the idea of using robotics to improve disaster response. We were inspired by news and case studies showing that delays and accessibility issues during catastrophes often worsen injuries and fatalities. The first stage involved brainstorming engineering solutions that could reduce risks to human responders, such as ground rovers for hazardous environments and drones for rapid surveying.

We researched current rescue robotics and humanitarian logistics to identify gaps in cost, flexibility, and accessibility. Early prototypes focused on modular, low-cost designs, combining digital simulations with physical CAD modelling.

We used Python for simulations to model drone and rover behavior in complex scenarios, Onshape for designing mechanical components, and Blender for realistic 3D visualizations. Arduino programming allowed us to control physical prototypes. Each phase involved iterative improvement: testing ideas, receiving feedback, and refining our designs for practicality and safety.

Through this process, we balanced innovation with feasibility, ensuring our robots could function autonomously, handle obstacles, and provide real-time data while remaining affordable and educationally accessible.
