Tools and Digital Platforms

To build and present our project, we relied on a suite of digital tools. Python was used to create simulations of drones and rovers in disaster zones, enabling experimentation with navigation, coordination, and decision-making before building physical prototypes.

Blender helped us visualize 3D environments and robot interactions, while Onshape allowed collaborative cloud-based CAD design for mechanical components. Arduino was used to program our robots in C+, controlling motors, sensors, and autonomous behaviors.

We encountered challenges, such as simulation performance limits, file transfer between Blender and Onshape, and steep learning curves for new software. To overcome these, we simplified models, optimized assets, and learned advanced export settings. The benefits were significant: rapid iteration, realistic visualization, and a bridge between virtual simulation and real-world hardware.

Using these tools strengthened our technical and presentation skills, combining programming, mechanical design, and visualization into a cohesive project workflow.
